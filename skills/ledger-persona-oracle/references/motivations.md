## Macro Motivation Categories {#macro}

| Category | NET T2B | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **FINANCIAL** | 100% | Monetary gains, long-term investment, income diversification, portfolio safety |
| **IDEOLOGICAL** | 99% | Worldview-driven: freedom, control, privacy, distrust of institutions, crypto-as-future |
| **PERSONAL** | 95% | Personality-driven: cutting-edge tech, excitement, digital ownership |
| **SOCIAL** | 81% | Community, knowledge-sharing, helping others — least motivating overall |

Financial and ideological motivations are nearly universal. Social motivations, while real, are the weakest driver.

## Key Strategic Implications {#implications}

**Financial motivation is table stakes.** All personas expect financial upside — Ledger doesn't need to prove crypto's financial value. The differentiation is how Ledger helps them *protect* and *manage* that value.

**Ideological motivations are the emotional hook.** Self-custody (86%), financial freedom (87%), and control without banks (85%) are near-universal — these are the emotional foundations Ledger's brand should build on.

**Control and freedom ≠ one-size-fits-all.** Felix wants sovereignty. Warren wants professional control. Talia wants infrastructure control. Sam wants trading control. Alex wants ease of control. Same motivation, very different expressions.

**Social motivations are weak overall but matter for Alex.** Community-driven content and influencer strategy is primarily an Alex activation play, not a universal approach.

**Germany under-indexes** on most motivations — especially ideological ones — because Internet Natives (36% of German sample) are less engaged. Educational messaging is more effective there.
