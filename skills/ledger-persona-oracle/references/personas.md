## Felix — Freedom Advocate {#felix}

**Essence:** Cautious and conviction-led. Ideologically driven — seeks financial freedom, self-custody, and protection from instability. Distrusts traditional systems. Privacy is foundational, not optional.

**Size:** ~16% of sample | US: 16% | DE: 12% | BR: 21%

### Motivations (top 5)
- Financial freedom
- Protecting savings against inflation / economic instability
- Having full control over assets (self-custody)
- Long-term investment / store of value
- Keeping financial activities private / anonymous

### Key attitudes
- 88% want crypto activity to stay private and untraceable
- 76% say crypto security gives them real peace of mind
- 76% use crypto to shield savings from inflation

### Fears & frustrations
- Security risks (39%)
- Price volatility (32%)
- Uncertainty about long-term value (31%)
- Barrier to hardware wallet: fear of losing physical device (29%)

### What they want from a wallet
- Full control over private keys (44%)
- Offline keys (less vulnerable) (36%)
- Stronger security (35%)
- Transparency from provider
- Reputation & trustworthiness
- Easier payment options

### Priority: Security vs Convenience
35% security · 51% both · 13% convenience → Security-leaning

### Ledger adoption
Likely users; however less likely to own hardware wallets than expected — conversion is a priority.

### How to speak to Felix
- **Lead with sovereignty.** Position Ledger as trustworthy essentials for asset safety.
- **Highlight freedom.** Promote decentralisation and Ledger's difference from traditional systems.
- **Provide reassurance.** Practical tips on safe device use and avoiding key loss.
- Tone: principled, trustworthy, never corporate-sounding

### Typical voice
> "There is power in going anywhere and having access to that money and it's going to be worth the same thing regardless of where I go."

## Warren — Wealth Architect {#warren}

**Essence:** A deliberate investor modernising his financial legacy. Focused on portfolio diversification and disciplined wealth growth. Expects clarity, control, and professional-grade tools.

**Size:** ~18% of sample | US: 18% | DE: 19% | BR: 17%

### Motivations (top 5)
- Diversifying income
- Financial freedom
- Potential for high investment returns
- Build generational wealth / secure financial future
- Long-term investment / store of value

### Key attitudes
- 73% say crypto security gives real peace of mind
- 69% believe early entry gives higher rewards than traditional investing
- 67% want crypto activity to stay private and untraceable

### Fears & frustrations
- Price volatility
- Security risks
- Uncertainty about long-term value
- Barrier to hardware wallet: cost/price (13%), concerns about security/trustworthiness (12%)

### What they want from a wallet
- Transparency around fees, security, operations
- Reputation and trustworthiness of the brand
- Full control over private keys (self-custody)
- Seamless integration between secure tools and everyday digital platforms
- Increased privacy features
- Easier ways to use crypto for payments

### How to speak to Warren
- **Ensure transparency** around Ledger security, operations and additional fees.
- **Promote integration** between hardware wallets and digital platforms (apps, web).
- **Provide portfolio dashboards** — easy access to wealth information.
- Tone: professional, data-driven, no hype

### Typical voice
> "Now I look at it in long term, for retirement — I have my investments across cryptos and now I want to see them grow over time."

## Talia — Tech Futurist {#talia}

**Essence:** Lives in the future. Fluent in protocols, DAOs, and decentralised identity. Crypto isn't just something she holds — it's where she builds, earns, and connects. Privacy and control over data matter deeply.

**Size:** ~23% of sample (largest individual segment)

### Motivations (top)
- Crypto represents the future of digital currency
- Potential for high investment returns
- Digital ownership (NFTs, on-chain identity)
- Diversifying investment portfolio
- Having full control over assets (self-custody)
- Exploring cutting-edge technology (Web3, blockchain)
- Belief in decentralisation

### Key attitudes
- Strong believer in Web3 as infrastructure, not just investment
- Actively interested in DeFi (staking, lending, yield farming)
- Values privacy and digital identity control as much as financial outcomes

### What they want
- Innovation, not feature parity
- Tools that integrate with DeFi, DAOs, dApps
- Control over personal digital data and identity
- Privacy features (zero-knowledge proofs, anonymous transactions)
- Cutting-edge security architecture

### How to speak to Talia
- **Position Ledger as the trust layer** for builders and the wider ecosystem.
- **Lead with the roadmap** — show what's coming, not just what exists.
- **Speak protocol.** She's fluent; don't dumb it down.
- **Highlight Assets–Access–Identity** roadmap.
- Tone: technical, forward-looking, peer-to-peer (not brand-to-consumer)

### Strategic value
Innovation north star. Early adopters who shape the product roadmap. Winning Talia cements Ledger as the default trust layer for builders.

## Sam — Savvy Trader {#sam}

**Essence:** High-frequency, high-intent. Wants to keep his gains in reach — not on exchanges. Chases opportunity but not blindly — tactical, experienced, tools-motivated. Blends short-term execution with long-term value thinking.

**Size:** ~27% of sample | US: 15% | DE: 20% | BR: 10% | (14% total after weighting)

### Motivations (top 5)
- Control over money without banks
- Faster or cheaper ways to send payments
- Crypto = future of digital currency / how we pay
- Build generational wealth / secure financial future
- Financial freedom

### Key attitudes
- 88% enjoy the fast-paced and dynamic nature of the crypto market
- 86% actively seek better ways to control personal data and identity online
- 84% want crypto activity to stay private and untraceable

### Fears & frustrations
- Price volatility (30%)
- Fear of losing access (22%)
- Security risks (21%)
- Barrier to hardware wallet: trusts current method (12%), fear of losing recovery phrase (11%), fear of losing device (10%)

### What they want from a wallet
- Full control over private keys (41%)
- To more securely manage digital identity (31%)
- Stronger security (30%)
- Reasonable prices
- Seamless integration
- Easy and secure recovery options

### Priority: Security vs Convenience
37% convenience · 29% both · 24% security → Convenience-leaning

### Ledger adoption
14% Ledger users, 20% considerers. Biggest share of daily Ledger Live activity. Strongest purchase intent for new features.

### How to speak to Sam
- **Promote hardware:** Ledger as better and more secure than software wallets.
- **Risk transparency:** Educate so he can make faster, informed decisions.
- **Push Ledger Live** as a safe, convenient trading hub.
- **Be timely** — he acts on opportunity, so messaging must be in the moment.
- Tone: direct, practical, no fluff, respects his experience

### Typical voice
> "I am just trying to find a way to make ends meet and getting into crypto has enabled me to do that."

## Alex — Internet Native {#alex}

**Essence:** Early in her crypto journey but far from passive. Influenced by culture, friends, TikTok, viral trends. She's more active than she looks — just underconfident. Wants in but needs someone to make it feel safe and simple.

**Size:** ~28% of sample (largest group by count) | US: 28% | BR: 25% | DE: 36%

### Motivations (top 5)
- Crypto = future of digital currency and payments
- Potential for high investment returns
- Digital ownership (NFTs)
- Diversify investment portfolio
- Having full control over assets (self-custody)

### Key attitudes
- 49% worried about theft of personal data online
- 46% actively seek better ways to control personal data
- 46% prioritise encrypted communication

### Fears & frustrations
- Security risks (24%)
- Price volatility (19%)
- Online data privacy (19%)
- Barrier to hardware wallet: compatibility concerns (14%), cost (12%), lack of understanding (12%)

### What they want from a wallet
- Reasonable pricing
- Regular security updates
- Transparency around fees, security, operations
- Easier and more secure account/key recovery options
- Unified way to manage digital identity and personal data

### Priority: Security vs Convenience
36% convenience · 43% both · 22% security → Balanced, leans convenience

### How to speak to Alex
- **Keep it simple.** Avoid technical jargon — clarity wins.
- **Use social media and influencers** to educate and introduce Ledger.
- **Easy onboarding** — smooth UX, clear flows, card-first access.
- **Be a trusted guide**, not a brand pitching to her.
- Tone: warm, culturally aware, zero jargon, social-first

### Typical voice
> "I didn't want to miss out on the crypto wave — it felt like you are being part of a bigger community."
