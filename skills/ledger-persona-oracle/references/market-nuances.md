## Motivations by Market {#motivations}

### Brazil — top motivations
1. Potential for high investment returns (92%)
2. Financial freedom (95%) ← highest of 3 markets
3. Additional income diversification (92%)
4. Self-custody / full control (92%)
5. Diversify investment portfolio (94%)
6. Faster/cheaper payments — notably higher than US/DE

**Profile:** Brazil is the most ideologically and financially motivated market. Strong focus on financial freedom and self-custody, combined with need for alternative financial systems (banking structure concerns). Freedom Advocates more prevalent.

### Germany — top motivations
1. Protecting savings from inflation (81%) ← highest of 3 markets for this
2. Potential for high investment returns (84%)
3. Crypto = future of digital currency (80%)
4. Long-term investment (80%)
5. Self-custody (80%)

**Profile:** Germany is the least motivated market overall, largely because Internet Natives (less engaged, more socially motivated) are overrepresented. However, inflation protection is uniquely salient — likely tied to historical memory of hyperinflation. Lower scores on ideological motivations.

### United States — top motivations
1. Make money quickly (86%) ← highest of 3 markets
2. Build generational wealth (88%) ← highest
3. Financial freedom (86%)
4. Potential for high investment returns (90%)
5. Diversify income (88%)

**Profile:** US is most financially aggressive — highest score on quick profits and generational wealth. More open to high-risk/high-reward opportunities. More familiar and positive about self-custody.

## Self-Custody Familiarity & Sentiment by Market {#self-custody}

| Market | Familiarity | Positive sentiment | Key concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | Very high (>50% "very familiar") | Most positive | — |
| Brazil | Moderate | Less likely to see self-custody as secure | Need for reassurance |
| Germany | Lower | Lower positive sentiment | Complexity and unfamiliarity |

**Implication:** Ledger needs different entry points. US → reinforce confidence. Germany → educate from scratch. Brazil → reassure on security while leveraging strong ideological motivation.

## Hardware Wallet Barriers by Market {#hw-barriers}

### United States — top barriers
1. Fear of losing the recovery phrase (24%) ← highest
2. Nothing/planning to get one soon (12%)
3. Fear of losing the physical device (14%)

### Brazil — top barriers
1. Fear of losing the physical device (19%) ← highest
2. Nothing/planning to get one soon (14%)
3. Concerns about security (13%)
4. Concerns about compatibility (12%)

### Germany — top barriers
1. Cost/price (12%) ← price sensitivity highest here
2. Haven't researched (12%) ← knowledge gap
3. Fear of losing the physical device (11%)

**Key insight:** Germany's barrier is primarily informational (cost perception + lack of research). Brazil's barrier is emotional (fear of physical loss and security doubts). US's barrier is the recovery phrase — a specific onboarding challenge.

## Risk & Security Preferences by Market {#risk}

| Market | Security priority | Convenience priority | Risk tolerance |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | Highest | — | High risk tolerance (more high-risk/high-reward seekers) |
| Germany | Lower than US | Higher | More moderate |
| Brazil | — | — | Safer investments preferred |

**Wallet feature priorities:**
- US: Security track record · Regular security updates · Full user control over private keys
- Germany: Regular security updates · Ease of set-up · Transparency from provider
- Brazil: Reputation and trustworthiness · Transparency from provider · Full user control over private keys

**Germany wants ease of set-up as a top feature** — confirms that complexity is a key barrier.

## Strategic Recommendations by Market {#strategy}

### Brazil
- Emphasise **empowerment and control** — ideological motivations are strong
- Address security concerns directly (hacking, scams are top fears)
- Highlight Secure Element chip, Ledger Live security, recovery options
- **Persona focus:** Felix (freedom + self-custody messaging)

### Germany
- **Education first** — lower self-custody familiarity and more Internet Natives
- Targeted beginner guides, localised content, trusted local institution partnerships
- Reduce perceived complexity and price concerns
- **Persona focus:** Alex (education, simplicity, social validation)

### United States
- **Confidence and capability** — users are most ready for self-custody
- Emphasise advanced features, trading tools, Ledger Live premium
- Address recovery phrase anxiety specifically in onboarding
- **Persona focus:** Sam (trading tools), Warren (portfolio management), Talia (advanced features)
